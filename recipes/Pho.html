Pho

Description:

Experience the heartwarming flavors of Vietnam with this authentic pho recipe. This traditional Vietnamese noodle soup is a harmonious blend of aromatic spices, savory broth, tender rice noodles, and your choice of protein, creating a bowl of pure comfort. Our recipe starts with a slow-simmered broth infused with star anise, cinnamon, cloves, and ginger, delivering a depth of flavor that's both complex and comforting. Combined with fresh herbs like cilantro and Thai basil, crisp bean sprouts, and a squeeze of lime, every bite bursts with vibrant, aromatic delight.

Whether you're a seasoned pho enthusiast or new to this iconic dish, this recipe makes it easy to recreate the magic at home. Perfect for a cozy dinner or a crowd-pleasing gathering, this customizable recipe lets you choose from beef, chicken, or tofu, making it versatile for all preferences. Serve your pho piping hot, accompanied by traditional garnishes like hoisin sauce and sriracha for that extra kick. With every slurp, you'll savor a taste of Vietnam's rich culinary heritage.

Ingredients:

For Broth

  • Beef bones or chicken bones (for a rich, flavorful base)
  • Water
  • Onion (charred or roasted)
  • Ginger (charred or roasted)
  • Star anise
  • Cinnamon sticks
  • Cloves
  • Cardamom pods (optional)
  • Fish sauce
  • Sugar (rock sugar preferred)
  • Salt

For Noodles

  • Rice noodles (banh pho), medium-sized

Protien

  • Thinly sliced beef (e.g., brisket, eye of round, or flank steak)
  • Meatballs (optional)
  • Chicken (for chicken pho or pho ga)
  • Tofu (for a vegetarian option)

Toppings

  • Fresh bean sprouts
  • Thai basil
  • Cilantro
  • Green onions
  • Lime wedges
  • Sliced chili peppers (e.g., Thai bird chili or jalape√±o)
  • Hoisin sauce (for dipping or adding to broth)
  • Sriracha (for a spicy kick)

Steps:

  1. Prepare the Broth: Roast the bones, onion, and ginger until fragrant. Simmer with spices (star anise, cinnamon, cloves) for 4-6 hours.
  2. Cook the Noodles: Soak or boil rice noodles according to the package instructions. Drain and set aside.
  3. Prepare the Toppings: Wash and chop fresh herbs. Slice lime and chili peppers. Set aside bean sprouts.
  4. Assemble the Pho: Place cooked noodles in a bowl. Add thinly sliced beef or chicken. Ladle the hot broth over the ingredients to cook the meat.
  5. Garnish and Serve: Top with fresh herbs, bean sprouts, lime juice, and chili. Add hoisin sauce or sriracha for extra flavor.
